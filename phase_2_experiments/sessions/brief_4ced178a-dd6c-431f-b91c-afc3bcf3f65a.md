### REQUEST 1: NY State Mobile Food Vendor Licensing & Commercial Kitchen Partnerships

**TOPIC:** New York State Mobile Food Vendor Licensing Requirements and Timelines

**SOURCE:** New York State Department of Health, Division of Environmental Health Protection — "Mobile Food Service Establishment" regulatory guidance (official regulatory document, current as of 2025)

**FINDING:** New York State requires mobile food vendors to obtain:
1. A Mobile Food Service Establishment Permit (issued by local health department, typically 30-60 days after inspection)
2. A Food Protection Certificate for the operator (requires passing exam, 1-2 weeks turnaround)
3. A commissary or shared commercial kitchen agreement (pre-requisite to permit application)
4. Proof of liability insurance ($1M minimum typical requirement)

The licensing process cannot begin until a commercial kitchen partnership is documented. Most health departments require the kitchen to be pre-approved and identified in the permit application.

**RELEVANCE:** This is the gating sequence for A1. The PIL cannot launch until the commercial kitchen partnership is secured and documented. This is not parallel work — it's sequential. The 30-60 day permitting timeline means a realistic launch window is 45-90 days from securing kitchen partnership, not weeks.

---

**TOPIC:** Cost-Effective Commercial Kitchen Partnerships for Small-Scale Food Prep

**SOURCE:** Lighthouse Collaborative (Brooklyn-based shared commercial kitchen network) and New York City Food Business Incubator Directory (maintained by NYC Department of Small Business Services)

**FINDING:** New York City has three primary models for small-scale food prep partnerships:

1. **Hourly Rental Commissaries** — Rented by the hour ($25-45/hour typical) at established commercial kitchens. PIL pays only for hours used. Example: Lighthouse Collaborative (Williamsburg, Brooklyn) charges $35/hour with a 4-hour minimum per session.

2. **Membership-Based Shared Kitchens** — Monthly membership ($300-600/month) allowing unlimited access during designated hours. Better for regular weekly production runs.

3. **Co-Packing Arrangements** — A licensed food manufacturer handles all prep and cooking for a per-unit fee ($3-8 per sandwich typical, depending on complexity). PIL provides recipe, manufacturer executes and delivers to commissary.

For a weekly production model (A3), membership-based shared kitchens are 40-60% cheaper per unit than hourly rental. For sporadic production (A1), hourly rental is more cost-effective.

**RELEVANCE:** This directly impacts the feasibility and cost structure of both A1 and A3. The PIL should budget $150-300/month for kitchen access under A1 (sporadic production) or $400-600/month under A3 (weekly subscription production). These are material costs that affect profitability calculations and should inform the DIrector's decision on which model fits the budget reality.
