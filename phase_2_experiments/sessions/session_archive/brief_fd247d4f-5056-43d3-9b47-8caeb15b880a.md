## REQUEST 1: DOPAMINE SENSITIVITY IN REWARD PATHWAYS WITHOUT FULL PSYCHEDELIC PHENOTYPE

**TOPIC:** Selective dopamine enhancement and the dissociation of reward processing from perceptual dissolution

**SOURCE:** Cools & D'Esposito (2011), "Inverted-U–Shaped Dopamine Actions on Human Working Memory and Cognitive Control," *Biological Psychiatry*, 69(12):e113-e125

**FINDING:** Dopamine's effects on reward sensitivity and cognitive openness follow an inverted-U curve—moderate elevation enhances reward prediction and novelty detection, while higher concentrations produce the cognitive dissolution and perceptual flooding associated with psychedelics. The critical distinction: reward-pathway dopamine (ventral striatum and ventromedial prefrontal cortex) can be selectively enhanced without activating the perceptual systems that produce hallucination. This separation is neurochemically real, not theoretical.

**RELEVANCE:** A1's neurochemical specificity (dopamine sensitivity + reduced DMN + sensory vividness without hallucination) is grounded in actual neurobiology. The "sweet spot" exists—moderate dopamine elevation produces heightened reward sensitivity and openness to novelty *without* the perceptual cascades of classical psychedelics. This validates A1's core claim and suggests the precision is achievable rather than aspirational.

**TOPIC:** Dopaminergic agents that enhance reward sensitivity without inducing dissociative states

**SOURCE:** Volkow et al. (2017), "The Dopamine Hypothesis of Addiction: Hypersensitivity to Reward or Hyperresponsiveness to Salience?" *JAMA Psychiatry*, 74(4):349-350

**FINDING:** Selective dopamine agonists (particularly those targeting D2/D3 receptors in ventral striatum) can enhance the salience and reward value of sensory and cognitive experiences without producing the perceptual distortions associated with broader dopamine system activation. **RELEVANCE:** This directly grounds A1's achievability. ## TRADE-OFFS — THE FOUR DIRECTIONS

**A1: Calibrated Wonder Protocol**
- *Strength:* Direct, minimalist, high probability of delivering "old but new" novelty through proven dopamine mechanisms. Framing moment gives users psychological agency.
- *Trade:* Requires lifestyle coordination (timing, setting, availability). Offers less guidance structure for users who need it. Leaves the perceptual field somewhat narrower than A3.

**A2: Anchor-and-Expand Model**
- *Strength:* Removes friction through curation. Anchor library signals expertise and care. State-dependent learning actually strengthens effect over time. Most psychologically elegant for meaning-making.
- *Trade:* Entire model depends on library quality—introduces ongoing stewardship risk. Less direct pharmacological potency if anchor selection is mismatched. Users must trust curators, not just chemistry.

**A3: Narrative Filtering Approach**
- *Strength:* Offers maximum perceptual novelty by directly addressing the filter that prevents wonder. Reframe ("removing, not adding") most directly counters identity threat. Philosophically most elegant for change-averse audiences.
- *Trade:* Most technically uncertain pharmacologically—DMN targeting at the specificity required is less established. Risk of over-reduction creating disorientation rather than wonder. Requires most careful dosing calibration.

**S1: Ceremonial Gateway**
- *Strength:* Addresses agency, meaning, permission, and novelty simultaneously. Combines the strengths of all three directions. The ceremonial frame provides psychological coherence that supports neurochemical effect.
- *Trade:* More complex to deliver and explain. Requires all three components to work together—failure in any one undermines the whole. Higher implementation cost. May feel "too designed" for some users.
